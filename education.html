Washington University in St. Louis 2012-2016

I am a Junior double majoring in Applied Mathematics and Comparitive Arts with a minor in general Fine Arts. I have been involved in a number of groups on campus including Alpha Rho Chi (a professional-social fraternity for architecture and the allied arts), Stressbusters, the climbing club, and extensively in Student Union. Education is not just about the grade for me, but the experience and sense of fulfillment you get after understanding a challenging topic, completeing a large project, and trying something out of your comfort zone. WashU has allowed me to delve into topics and build relationships with different people that I otherwise would have never encountered. The primary reasons I decided to come to WashU were to experience a greater breadth of diversity and to have the chance at a cross curriculum education that allowed me to delve into various studies. I began as a dual degree student studying Mechanical Engineering and Fine Art. Were it not for the fluidity WashU allows between its schools, I would not have been able to figure out where I felt I fit best or discover some of my current interests, such as German and coding.
